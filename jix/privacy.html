Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 24, 2026

Overview

Jix is an iPhone puzzle app developed by Dulio Denis. It works without an account and without collecting personal data. The short version: your game lives on your device.

Information stored on your device

To make the game work, Jix stores the following locally on your iPhone:

  • Active puzzle progress, Daily completion history, results, current streak, and level
  • Your settings — theme, font, appearance, haptics, sound, and notification preferences

This information is used only to restore the app and show your progress and preferences. Jix does not provide accounts or cloud synchronization.

Information we do not collect

Jix does not collect, transmit, sell, or share personal information. No name, email address, location, contacts, photos, advertising identifiers, or other personal content are required to play.

Notifications

Jix does not request notification permission on first launch. After your first Daily puzzle, Jix may offer Daily reminders. If you accept or enable notifications in Settings, reminders are scheduled locally through Apple's notification system. You can disable them in Jix or in iOS Settings.

Network access and third-party services

Launch puzzle content is bundled with the app. Jix does not send gameplay or preference data to a server and contains no advertising, analytics, tracking, social, purchase, StoreKit, or third-party purchase SDKs.

Children's privacy

Jix does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children. The game requires no personal information to play.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. The latest version is always available at this page, and the "last updated" date below shows when it was most recently revised.
